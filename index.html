Slide 02 / Foundation

Why Most Goals Fail

Common Breakdown Points

  • Goals are too vague to verify.
  • No quantity or percentage is defined.
  • No evidence artifact is named.
  • No timeline is attached to completion.
  • Ownership and accountability are unclear.

What That Causes

  • Inconsistent coaching from leader to leader.
  • Inflated or deflated self-assessment scores.
  • Low confidence in evaluation fairness.
  • Annual review surprises and defensiveness.
Bottom line: If you cannot measure a goal monthly, you cannot coach it fairly.
Facilitator prompt: “Which failure point is most common in your team right now?”
Slide 03 / Standard

The Objective Goal Standard

Before approving a goal, require all four criteria below.

1) Number

How much, how many, or what percent completion?

2) Timeline

By what month/date should this be completed?

3) Evidence

What tangible proof will verify completion?

4) Ownership

Who is directly accountable for execution?

Approval rule: No number + no evidence = not ready.
Facilitator prompt: Ask leaders to name valid evidence examples for 3 real roles.
Slide 04 / Rewrite Exercise

Vague vs Objective Goal Rewrites

Vague

“I want to be more helpful to the team.”

Objective

“Lead two morning huddles by Month 4 and submit agendas + notes for each.”

Vague

“I will learn more about budgeting.”

Objective

“Attend two budget review meetings and submit written summaries by Month 3.”

Vague

“I should improve communication.”

Objective

“Send weekly project status by Friday 3:00 PM for six consecutive months.”

Live activity: Each leader rewrites one weak goal from their current team.
Facilitator prompt: “If someone else scored this goal, would they score it the same way?”
Slide 05 / Structure

Balanced Goal Design: 1/3 + 1/3 + 1/3

Mastery of Existing Skills (1/3)

Improve quality, speed, consistency, and stewardship in current responsibilities.

New Skill Development (1/3)

Build future capability through learning, projects, and cross-functional growth.

Culture and Character (1/3)

Demonstrate values in visible, measurable behaviors that strengthen the team.

Facilitator focus: Every employee should leave goal-setting with one approved goal in each pillar.
Slide 06 / Pillar 1

Mastery of Existing Skills

What This Pillar Measures

  • Execution reliability in core responsibilities
  • Quality and efficiency improvements
  • Consistency over time

Strong Goal Examples

  • Reduce report turnaround from 5 days to 3 by Month 4.
  • Maintain 100% on-time submissions for the full segment.
  • Create and publish one SOP for weekly inventory by Month 3.
Check question: Is this goal tied to a current-role KPI?
Facilitator prompt: “What current KPI most needs lift for this role?”
Slide 07 / Pillar 2

New Skill Development

What This Pillar Measures

  • Capability growth beyond current role baseline
  • Readiness for expanded responsibilities
  • Cross-training and learning application

Strong Goal Examples

  • Complete leadership module and submit certificate by Month 2.
  • Shadow Finance 4 hours monthly for 4 months.
  • Lead one 15-minute team training by Month 5.
Check question: Will this increase future role readiness in a visible way?
Facilitator prompt: “What evidence proves the new skill was applied, not just attended?”
Slide 08 / Pillar 3

Culture and Character

What This Pillar Measures

  • Values in action, not just intent
  • Team cohesion and encouragement behaviors
  • Contribution to trust and morale

Strong Goal Examples

  • Lead two team devotions/huddles during the segment.
  • Complete three cross-team encouragement check-ins monthly.
  • Implement one morale-strengthening action by Month 4.
Check question: Can this behavior be counted and evidenced each month?
Facilitator reminder: Culture goals still require number + timeline + proof.
Slide 09 / Goal Construction

Build Objective Sub-Tasks

By [date], I will [action + quantity], evidenced by [artifact], to support [team outcome].

Worked Example

By Month 3, I will submit 12 weekly check-in reports with 100% on-time delivery, evidenced by the submission log, to support reporting accuracy and leadership visibility.

Leader Activity (8 min)

  • Pick one real direct-report goal.
  • Break into 2-4 measurable sub-tasks.
  • Include quantity, date, and evidence for each.

Minimum Acceptance Standard

  • Every sub-task has clear completion proof.
  • Progress can be scored monthly on 1-10 scale.
  • Manager support needed is identified.
Facilitator prompt: “Can someone outside this team verify completion from the artifact alone?”
Slide 10 / Quality Control

Run the 4-Part Goal Quality Test

Evidence Test

If someone claims 100% complete, can they show proof?

Number Test

Does the goal include a specific count/percent?

Timeline Test

Is month/date for completion explicit?

Support Test

Is required supervisor support clearly identified?

Peer-review activity: Swap goal drafts, mark pass/fail for each test, and rewrite any failed line immediately.
Facilitator prompt: “Any fail means the goal is not approved yet.”
Slide 11 / Monthly Rhythm

Monthly 1-10 Scoring + Check-In Calibration

Check-In Flow

1) Employee self-rates each goal (1-10).
2) Employee presents evidence artifacts.
3) Supervisor affirms or redirects objectively.
4) Agree next-month actions and support.

Calibration Rule

If roughly half of measurable work is complete, score should be near 5, not 8 or 9.

Practice Scenario

  • Goal requires 6 completed client follow-ups by month-end.
  • Employee completed 3 with evidence logs.
  • Discuss: appropriate score and coaching response.
Facilitator prompt: “What evidence would justify moving a score by +2 points?”
